1. Introduction

Welcome to Recall, your AI-powered meeting transcription and intelligence platform. Recall helps you capture, analyze, and organize meeting conversations with advanced features including real-time transcription, speaker identification, automatic action item detection, and intelligent summaries.

Key Features:
  • Real-time speech-to-text transcription
  • AI-powered speaker identification and voice enrollment
  • Automatic highlight detection for important moments
  • Smart action item extraction and tracking
  • Meeting summaries and email-ready formats
  • Voice assistant for conversational queries
  • Replay functionality to review key moments
  • Personal dashboard for tracking your assignments
Recall works best with Google Chrome or Microsoft Edge browsers. Make sure you have a working microphone connected to your device.

2. Getting Started

2.1 System Requirements

  • Browser: Google Chrome (recommended), Microsoft Edge, or Safari
  • Microphone: Built-in or external microphone
  • Internet Connection: Required for AI features
  • Permissions: Microphone access must be granted

2.2 First Time Setup

1 Open the Application

Navigate to the Recall application in your web browser.

2 Grant Microphone Permission

When prompted, click "Allow" to grant microphone access. This is required for recording.

3 Enter Your Name (Optional)

In the Personal Dashboard section, enter your name to enable personalized action item tracking.

If you accidentally denied microphone permission, click the lock icon in your browser's address bar and enable microphone access, then refresh the page.

3. Recording & Transcription

3.1 Starting a Recording

1 Click "Start Transcription"

Find the large blue button in the Recording Controls section and click it to begin.

2 Check Status

The status indicator will show "Listening" with a pulsing animation when active.

3 Speak Clearly

Speak at a normal pace and volume. The transcript will appear in real-time on the left side.

3.2 Stopping a Recording

Click the red "Stop Transcription" button when you're finished. Your transcript will be saved and available for review.

3.3 Understanding the Transcript Display

The transcript shows:

  • Speaker Name: Color-coded badges for each speaker
  • Transcript Text: What was said
  • Timestamp: When it was said
  • Confidence Score: Accuracy percentage of the transcription

3.4 Clearing the Transcript

Click the "Clear Transcript" button to remove all entries and start fresh. This action cannot be undone.

Always export or save important transcripts before clearing!

4. Speaker Identification

4.1 Voice Enrollment

Voice Enrollment allows you to train the system to recognize specific speakers.

1 Open Voice Enrollment

Click the "Voice Enrollment" button in the Quick Actions section.

2 Enter Speaker Name

Type the name of the person who will be speaking.

3 Record Voice Sample

Click "Start Recording" and have the person speak for 3-5 seconds. They should say a complete sentence.

4 Save Profile

Click "Save Voice Profile" to store the voice pattern.

For best results, enroll speakers before starting the main meeting recording. Have each person speak clearly in a quiet environment.

4.2 Managing Speakers

In the Speaker Settings section, you can:

  • Set Expected Speakers: Tell the system how many people will be in the meeting
  • Rename Speakers: Update speaker names for detected voices
  • Reset Detection: Clear all speaker data and start fresh

5. Smart Highlights

5.1 What are Smart Highlights?

Smart Highlights automatically identify important moments in your conversation, including:

  • Decisions made
  • Questions asked
  • Key insights shared
  • Important announcements
  • Agreement or consensus reached

5.2 Viewing Highlights

Highlights appear in the Smart Highlights panel on the right side of the screen. Each highlight shows:

  • Type (Decision, Question, Key Insight)
  • The highlighted text
  • Who said it
  • When it was said

5.3 Replaying Highlights

Click the Replay button on any highlight to see the context around that moment in a detailed modal view.

5.4 Detection Keywords

Highlight Type Trigger Keywords
Decision decided, approve, agree, let's go with, final decision
Question Sentences ending with "?"
Key Insight important, critical, key, significant, must

6. Action Items

6.1 Automatic Detection

Recall automatically detects action items from your conversation. Action items are extracted when people mention tasks, assignments, or commitments.

1 Record Conversation

Action items are detected in real-time as you speak.

2 Click "Detect Action Items"

After your meeting, click this button in the Quick Actions section to analyze all action items.

3 Review Action Items

All detected action items appear in the Smart Highlights panel.

6.2 Personal Action Items

If you've entered your name, Recall will automatically identify action items assigned to you and show notifications.

Personal Action Item Features:
  • Real-time notifications when you're assigned a task
  • Personal dashboard showing all your action items
  • Status tracking (Not Started, In Progress, Completed)
  • Priority indicators

6.3 Managing Action Items

In your Personal Dashboard, you can:

  • View all action items assigned to you
  • Update status (click the status badge to cycle through options)
  • See who assigned each task
  • View when the task was mentioned
Action items work best when people clearly state assignments like "John, can you handle that?" or "Sarah will send the report."

7. Meeting Summaries

7.1 Generating Summaries

1 Complete Your Recording

Stop the transcription when your meeting is finished.

2 Click "Generate Summary"

Find this button in the Quick Actions section.

3 Wait for AI Processing

The system will analyze your transcript and create a comprehensive summary.

4 View Summary

The Meeting Summary modal will open with your formatted summary.

7.2 Summary Contents

Meeting summaries include:

  • Meeting Overview: Key topics discussed
  • Main Points: Important discussions and decisions

Click the "Copy Summary" button to copy the entire summary to your clipboard. You can then paste it into emails, documents, or your note-taking app.

13. Troubleshooting

13.1 Microphone Not Working

If the microphone isn't working:
  • Check browser permissions (click lock icon in address bar)
  • Ensure microphone is properly connected
  • Try a different browser (Chrome or Edge recommended)
  • Check system microphone settings
  • Refresh the page and grant permission again

13.2 Poor Transcription Quality

If transcripts are inaccurate:

  • Reduce background noise: Find a quiet environment
  • Speak clearly: Enunciate words at a moderate pace
  • Check microphone: Position microphone closer to speakers
  • One speaker at a time: Avoid overlapping conversation
  • Good internet connection: Ensure stable connection

13.3 Speaker Identification Issues

If speakers aren't being identified correctly:

  • Enroll speakers before starting the meeting
  • Ensure voice samples are clear and at least 3-5 seconds long
  • Set the correct number of expected speakers
  • Re-record voice profiles if needed
  • Manually rename speakers in Speaker Settings

13.4 Summary Generation Failed

If summaries won't generate:

  • Ensure you have recorded at least some content
  • Check your internet connection
  • Try again after a few moments
  • Clear transcript and start fresh if issues persist
